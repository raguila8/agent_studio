      Most home emergencies are small failures with large downstream consequences. The repair on the original [plumbing line](/services/plumbing-hvac) is straightforward. The repair on the soaked subfloor, the warped cabinetry, and the mold that follows is not.

      Data from the [Insurance Information Institute](https://www.iii.org/) consistently puts water damage among the most common and most expensive homeowner claims, and the cost curve is steep. The same event handled in the first hour after detection looks very different than the same event handled in the first day. Sparking outlets and burning smells are the most time-sensitive of all, because the failure mode behind them is a fire that has not started yet, and they need a licensed [electrician](/services/electrical) on site immediately.

By the time the technician arrives, the situation is stable, the diagnosis is clean, and the repair scope quoted is what is actually broken, not the larger scope that would have existed if you had waited until morning.

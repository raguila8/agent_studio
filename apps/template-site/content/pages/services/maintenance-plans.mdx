    heading: |
      Why *scheduled maintenance* lowers lifetime cost
    intro: 'Maintenance is not a hedge. It is the cheapest way to keep major home systems performing within their rated specifications for the full length of their service life.'
    body: |
      Every major appliance and system in a home, including furnaces, air conditioners, water heaters, and electrical panels, is engineered around the assumption that it will be serviced periodically. Manufacturer warranties, service-life expectations, and [ENERGY STAR](https://www.energystar.gov/) efficiency ratings are all calculated that way. A system that goes unmaintained is not running fine without help. It is operating outside the conditions the manufacturer used to set every published number about it.

      The performance drop happens gradually. An air conditioner with a dirty coil and undercharged refrigerant might cool the house, but it is running longer cycles to do it, drawing more current than it should, and stressing the compressor in a way that shortens its life. The same drift happens on the heating side of an [HVAC system](/services/plumbing-hvac) with a clogged heat exchanger. Annual tune-ups exist specifically to catch these drifts before they show up in your utility bill or your equipment lifespan.

      The early-detection value is even larger. A licensed technician opening up a system once a year sees the early stages of failures that homeowners cannot see at all: corroded heat exchangers, weakening capacitors, sediment buildup in water heaters, loose lugs in [electrical panels](/services/electrical). These problems are cheap to fix when caught and expensive when they cascade.

    faqs:
      - question: How much can a maintenance plan actually save me?
        answer: |
          The honest answer is that savings depend on the age and condition of your equipment. For homeowners with mid-life HVAC systems, water heaters, and panels, plan members typically save the cost of the plan within the first year through priority scheduling, discounted repairs, and avoided emergency calls. For brand-new systems, the savings are smaller in the early years and grow over time.
      - question: What if I cancel?
        answer: |
          Plans are month-to-month after the first year. Cancel anytime with no penalty and no automatic renewal traps. The price you sign up at is the price you pay until you choose to change plans.
      - question: Do I have to use you for repairs if I have a maintenance plan?
        answer: |
          No. The plan covers your tune-ups and entitles you to member rates if you do use us. There is no obligation to hire us for any specific repair found during a tune-up, and your tune-up still happens whether you do or not.
      - question: What is not covered by the plan?
        answer: |
          The plan covers inspections, tune-ups, and discounted repair rates. It does not cover the cost of major replacements, new installs, or work outside the systems listed in your plan. The exact scope is laid out in writing before you sign up so there are no surprises.
      - question: Can I add my plumbing or electrical to an HVAC plan later?
        answer: |
          Yes. Most homeowners start with HVAC and add plumbing or electrical in later years. We make it simple to expand the plan and the visit schedule adjusts automatically the following year.
      - question: Do maintenance visits actually extend equipment life?
        answer: |
          Manufacturers publish service-life expectations based on regular maintenance. Equipment that goes years without a tune-up tends to underperform that expectation and fail earlier. A well-maintained furnace, AC, or water heater can outlast its rated life by several years; a neglected one rarely makes it to the warranty expiration.

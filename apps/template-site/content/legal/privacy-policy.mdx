Ridgeway Home Services is a fictional business used to demonstrate this website template. This sample Privacy Policy describes the kinds of information a typical home services contractor website may collect, how that information may be used, and what choices visitors typically have. A real privacy policy must be tailored to the actual practices, jurisdictions, and integrations of the operating business.

This sample policy is for general website and inquiry information only. A real contractor business may also handle customer data, payment information, and project records that fall under additional state, federal, or contractual requirements.

## Information We Collect

A typical home services contractor website may collect the following types of information:

* **Information you provide directly.** This may include your name, email address, phone number, service address, message content, project details, and any other information you choose to share when you request an estimate or contact the business.
* **Estimate and scheduling information.** If you request a quote or schedule a visit through the website, you may be asked to provide information needed to dispatch a technician or prepare an estimate. Scheduling and estimate workflows may be handled through one or more third-party platforms.
* **Basic website information.** Like most websites, hosting and security tools may collect technical information such as your IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, referring website, and the date and time of your visit.
* **Email, text, or phone communications.** If you contact the business by phone, email, or text, a record of that communication may be kept so the team can respond and provide follow-up.

Please do not send urgent safety information, emergency requests, or highly sensitive personal information through this website or by ordinary email. For active emergencies (gas leak, active fire risk, sewage flooding, structural collapse), call your local emergency number or relevant utility hotline.

## How We Use Information

A typical home services contractor would use personal information only for appropriate business purposes, including to:

* respond to estimate requests and contact submissions;
* schedule, confirm, or follow up about service visits and installs;
* provide information about available services and maintenance plans;
* operate, maintain, secure, and improve the website;
* protect against spam, misuse, or unauthorized access;
* comply with applicable legal, regulatory, licensing, or insurance requirements.

A real client policy should specify whether the business sells personal information and how it treats targeted advertising or data brokerage. This sample assumes the business does not sell personal information.

## Online Scheduling and Third-Party Services

A real client policy should list the actual third-party platforms used for scheduling, estimates, payments, communications, and analytics. Information submitted through any third-party platform is collected and processed by that platform according to its own terms and privacy practices, and may also be shared with the contractor for service and account management purposes.

A real business may also use trusted service providers for website hosting, email, SMS, payments, communications, security, and similar operational support. Those providers should be limited to using information only as needed to provide services or as otherwise required by law.

## Customer Records and Project Information

Once you become a customer, information related to your service address, project history, payment records, and warranty documentation may be retained for the period required by the business, by law, by insurance, and by manufacturer warranty terms. A real client policy should specify retention periods and customer rights.

## Cookies and Website Technologies

This website may use cookies or similar technologies that are necessary for basic site functionality, security, performance, or content management. You can set your browser to block or delete cookies, but parts of the website may not work as intended.

If analytics, advertising, or session-replay tools are added to the website, the policy should be updated to describe how those tools are used, what data they collect, and how visitors can opt out.

## How We Share Information

A typical home services contractor may share information only when reasonably necessary, including:

* with service providers who help operate the website, scheduling, payments, communications, or business systems;
* with subcontractors or suppliers involved in completing a specific project, when necessary for the work;
* when required by law, subpoena, court order, licensing board request, or other legal process;
* when needed to protect the rights, safety, or security of customers, employees, the business, the website, or others;
* with your consent or at your direction.

A real policy should specify whether information is shared with third parties for their own marketing purposes. This sample assumes it is not.

## Data Security

A real business should use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information. No website, email system, or internet transmission can be guaranteed to be completely secure, so please use discretion when deciding what information to send electronically.

## Your Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict use of personal information you have submitted. A real policy should specify how to exercise those rights and how the business will verify the request.

You may also ask the business to stop sending non-essential communications. The business may still contact you when needed for active service appointments, billing, warranty, legal, or administrative reasons.

## Children's Privacy

This website is intended for a general adult audience and is not directed to children under 13. A real business should not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through the website without appropriate parent or guardian involvement.

## Links to Other Websites

This website may link to third-party websites, including scheduling platforms, payment processors, manufacturer support pages, and social media profiles. The contractor is not responsible for the privacy practices, security, or content of those third-party websites. Review their privacy policies before submitting information to them.

## Changes to This Policy

A real business may update its Privacy Policy from time to time. When changes are made, the "Last updated" date above should be revised. Continued use of the website after changes are posted typically means the updated policy applies.
